The Beverly Hymn

An original song for small cups, brave mouths, and the moment a room realizes the prank has become a lifestyle.

Original Institute Chant

"Beverly, Beverly, Bitter And Bright"

Beverly, Beverly, bitter and bright,
Clear little comet in fluorescent light.
You came from Italy, tiny and dry,
Made every tourist reconsider pie.

Beverly, Beverly, hold up the cup,
Everyone laughs until everyone looks up.
Sweetness is easy and cola is kind,
Beverly knocks on the back of the mind.

Pour me an ounce and a lecture on rind,
Give me the truth they politely declined.
If I make a face, let history see:
The face was the price of becoming more free.

Beverly, Beverly, bitter and bright,
Good morning, grapefruit. Good evening, spite.
We sip, we bow, we stagger, we grin,
Then send the next unsuspecting friend in.
